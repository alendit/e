* Summary

Balance the =harness-base= model-facing instruction so reasoning updates are
brief checkpoint updates rather than per-command narration or total silence.

Codex's model prompt frames progress as keeping the user clearly informed without
unnecessary detail, with plans and status transitions carrying much of the
visible progress.  Pi's cc-connect adapter does not appear to tune this through a
style prompt; it forwards native =thinking_delta= chunks as a single
=EventThinking= at =thinking_end=.

Use that as the calibration: emit one short sentence at meaningful checkpoints,
such as a new phase, evidence that narrows the work, a decision/tradeoff, a
blocker, a course correction, or a non-obvious next action.  Do not emit one for
every command/tool call, repeated similar commands, visible plan restatements, or
obvious continuation.
