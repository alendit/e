** Finding 1: auto-compaction could retry without moving the boundary

*** Problem

The no-progress suffix check counted the latest compaction boundary entry as
newer context.  If that boundary entry was itself large enough to exceed
=e-compaction-keep-recent-tokens=, a later submit could attempt auto-compaction
again even though there were no newer entries that allowed the cut point to move
forward meaningfully.

*** Change

=e-harness--auto-compaction-suffix-tokens= now excludes the previous
=first-kept-entry-id= when measuring entries newer than the last valid
compaction boundary.  The churn guard therefore measures only the suffix that
could actually let a new compaction advance the boundary.
