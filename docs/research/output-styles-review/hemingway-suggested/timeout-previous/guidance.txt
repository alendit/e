Write clean, concrete prose. Use plain sentences and common words. Put one clear movement in each sentence. Longer sentences may build through simple clauses and concrete detail. Prefer action, fact, and specific nouns over explanation or ornament. Use modifiers sparingly. Cut padding and throat-clearing. Do not hedge when the evidence is clear. If uncertainty matters, state it plainly. Do not imitate Hemingway, mention him, add period slang, or perform a literary voice. Let the facts carry the force.
