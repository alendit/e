#+title: Hemingway Corpus Style Analysis
#+startup: overview

* Scope

This analysis uses the larger EPUB corpus at =docs/research/output-styles-review/sources/hemingway.epub=.
The goal is to compare =hemingway-current= with =hemingway-suggested-v2=.
The comparison asks which guidance better matches Hemingway's prose without asking agents to cosplay it.
The analysis also asks which guidance is more useful for E's technical agent prose.

* Corpus shape

The fiction-only corpus has 908,391 words.
It has 61,570 sentences.
Its average sentence length is 14.75 words.
Its median sentence length is 11 words.
Sentences of 8 words or fewer make up 35.6 percent of the corpus.
Sentences of 25 words or more make up 14.4 percent of the corpus.
The average word length is 3.88 letters.
The word =and= appears 34.9 times per 1000 words.
The word =because= appears 0.92 times per 1000 words.
=-ly= adverbs appear 7.93 times per 1000 words.
Abstract suffix words appear 5.06 times per 1000 words.
Weak or simple verbs appear 62.3 times per 1000 words.
Copulas appear 35.3 times per 1000 words.
Quoted sentences make up 49.7 percent of the fiction corpus.

* Corpus finding

The larger corpus confirms the earlier smaller-corpus finding.
Hemingway fiction is plain and short-worded.
It is not always short-sentenced.
It often builds through simple clauses joined by =and=.
It uses many simple verbs and copulas.
It is low in abstraction.
It lets concrete action and fact carry the line.

* Current prompt

The current prompt says this.

#+begin_quote
Write in a clean, strong prose style.
This is not a request to imitate Ernest Hemingway, mention his works, or cosplay his voice.
Use plain declarative sentences.
Put one idea in each.
Choose short, common words over long ones.
Prefer strong verbs to adjectives and adverbs.
Avoid flowery, ornate, or unnecessary language.
Cut every word that does not carry weight.
Do not hedge or pad.
Let the facts stand on their own.
#+end_quote

* Suggested v2 prompt

The suggested v2 prompt says this.

#+begin_quote
Write in a clean, strong prose style.
This is not a request to imitate Ernest Hemingway, mention his works, or cosplay his voice.
Use plain declarative sentences.
Put one clear claim or action in each sentence.
Choose short, common words over long ones.
Prefer concrete facts and direct verbs over abstraction, adjectives, and adverbs.
Avoid flowery, ornate, or unnecessary language.
Cut padding and throat-clearing.
Do not hedge when the evidence is clear.
If uncertainty matters, state it plainly.
Let the facts carry the force.
#+end_quote

* Comparison

Suggested v2 matches the corpus better.
The main reason is the verb line.
The phrase =strong verbs= is too vague.
The corpus has many plain verbs.
Examples include =was=, =were=, =had=, =said=, =went=, =came=, =saw=, and =looked=.
These are not strong in a showy sense.
They are direct.
They say what happened.
Suggested v2 captures that better with =direct verbs=.
Suggested v2 also adds the missing corpus trait of concrete facts.
That matters because fiction-only abstraction markers are low.
Hemingway's force comes less from vivid verbs than from concrete nouns, plain action, and low explanation.

* Where current is less faithful

The current prompt says =Put one idea in each=.
The corpus does not fully support that.
There are many long sentences built from simple joined clauses.
The common pattern is =X and Y and Z and then A and B=.
That pattern is not ornate.
It is plain accumulation.
Suggested v2 improves this by saying =Put one clear claim or action in each sentence=.
That remains useful for agents.
It is also less false as a corpus claim.
The current prompt also says =Cut every word that does not carry weight=.
That is a good editing rule.
It is too absolute as a corpus description.
The corpus uses repetition and rhythm.
Suggested v2 is better because it says =Cut padding and throat-clearing=.
That targets the agent failure mode without banning useful repetition.

* Where current may still work better for agents

The current prompt has more force.
It is stricter.
That strictness helped the review-generation task.
It pushed the model toward short claims and low hedging.
Suggested v2 is more accurate to the corpus.
It may be slightly softer as an instruction.
The tradeoff is clear.
Current has stronger operational pressure.
Suggested v2 has better descriptive fit and safer technical wording.

* Verdict

Use =hemingway-suggested-v2= as the better style definition candidate.
It matches the larger Hemingway corpus better.
It says =concrete facts=.
It says =direct verbs=.
It avoids unnecessary abstraction.
It preserves truthful uncertainty.
It cuts padding rather than all repetition.
It avoids the misleading phrase =strong verbs=.
Current remains competitive if the only goal is forcing terse agent behavior.
Suggested v2 is the better next candidate if the goal is both corpus fidelity and agent behavior.
